Financial results for the 10 months ended January 31, 2014
**********************************************************

     The Government announced today (February 28) its financial results for the 10 months ended January 31, 2014.

     There was a surplus of HK$38.4 billion in the month of January, thereby bringing a cumulative surplus of HK$69.8 billion from April 1, 2013 to end of January, 2014. Expenditure for the 10-month period amounted to HK$328.2 billion and revenue HK$398 billion.

     The fiscal reserves stood at HK$803.7 billion as at January 31, 2014.

     A government spokesperson said that the surplus in January was mainly due to the receipt of salaries tax and profits tax. The bulk of the income for the year from key revenue sources such as profits tax, salaries tax and investment income has already been received. Taking into account various expenditure amounting to more than HK$100 billion in February and March, there will be deficits for those two months. The surplus for the year would decrease to HK$12 billion. The fiscal reserves would be HK$745.9 billion as at end March 2014.
