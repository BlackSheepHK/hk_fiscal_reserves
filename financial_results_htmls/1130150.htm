Financial results for the seven months ended October 31, 1999

*********************************************************

The Government announced today (Tuesday) its financial results for the seven months ended October 31, 1999.

Expenditure for the period April to October 1999 amounted to $128,548.8 million and revenue to $78,493.4 million, resulting in a deficit of $50,055.4 million.

The fiscal reserves stood at $384,247 million at October 31, a decrease of $50,055.4 million compared with the balance of $434,302.4 million at March 31, 1999, the end of the previous financial year.

On the deficit, a Government spokesman explained that since the Government's accounts were prepared on a cash basis, they reflected the incidence of receipts and payments during the period.

"The bulk of collections from Profits Tax and Salaries Tax were normally received in the latter part of the year, with roughly 85% of Profits Tax in the last five months and 80% of Salaries Tax in the last three months," he said.

"Therefore, it was not unusual, even in good years, to see a deficit at this time of the financial year," he added.
