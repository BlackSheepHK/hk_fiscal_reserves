Government announces financial results

**************************************

As a further step towards meeting the International Monetary Fund's Special Data Dissemination Standard (IMF SDDS), the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, from now on, will release a monthly summary of its financial results.

Details in respect of the financial results for the six months ended September 30, 1998 is set out below:

Expenditure for the year to date amounts to $115,202 million and revenue to $72,601.3 million, resulting in a deficit for the first six months of the financial year of $42,600.7 million. In addition, the net worth of the Land Fund has increased by $2,973.4 million since April 1, 1998.

As a result, the fiscal reserves stood at $417,915.7 million at September 30, 1998, a decrease of $39,627.3 million compared with the level at March 31, 1998, the end of the previous financial year.

Because the government's accounts are prepared on a cash basis and since the bulk of the collections from Profits Tax and Salaries Tax are normally received in the second half of the financial year (roughly 90% of Profits Tax and 80% of Salaries Tax), it is not unusual, even in good years, to see a substantial deficit in the first half of the financial year.
