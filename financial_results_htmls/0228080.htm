Financial results for the ten months ended January 31, 2004

*********************************************************

The Government announced today (February 28) its financial results for the ten months ended January 31, 2004.

Expenditure for the period April 2003 to January 2004 amounted to $202.9 billion and revenue to $157.7 billion, resulting in a deficit of $45.2 billion. There was a surplus of $25.8 billion in January.

A Government spokesman said that the surplus of $25.8 billion for the month of January 2004 reflected the salaries tax and profits tax collections in that month. He added that an up-to-date assessment of the likely deficit for the current financial year will be provided by the Financial Secretary in his Budget Speech on March 10, 2004.

The fiscal reserves stood at $270.3 billion at January 31, 2004.
