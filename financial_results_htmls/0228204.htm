Financial results for the ten months ended January 31, 2003

*********************************************************

The Government announced today (February 28) its financial results for the ten months ended January 31, 2003.

Expenditure for the period April 2002 to January 2003 amounted to $198.6 billion and revenue to $140.2 billion, resulting in a deficit of $58.4 billion. There was a surplus of $18.8 billion in January.

A Government spokesman said that the surplus of $18.8 billion for the month of January 2003 reflected the salary tax and profits tax collections in that month. He added that an up-to-date assessment of the likely deficit for the current financial year will be provided by the Financial Secretary in his Budget Speech on March 5, 2003.

The fiscal reserves stood at $314.1 billion at January 31, 2003.
