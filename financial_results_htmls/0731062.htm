Financial results for three months ended 30.6.99

************************************************

The Government announced today (Saturday) its financial results for the three months ended June 30, 1999.

Expenditure for the first three months of the current financial year amounted to $59,504.3 }million and revenue to $36,762.1 million, resulting in a deficit of $22,742.2 million.

The fiscal reserves stood at $411,560.2 million at June 30, a decrease of $22,742.2 million compared with the balance of $434,302.4 million at March 31, 1999, the end of the previous financial year.

A government spokesman said that the Government's accounts were prepared on a cash basis and reflected the incidence of receipts and payments during the period.

"The results remain in line with our expectations, based on the budgeted deficit for the year of $36.5 billion as announced by the Financial Secretary in his Budget Speech on March 3," he added.

More detailed figures are shown in Tables 1 and 2.
