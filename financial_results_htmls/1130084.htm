Financial results for the seven months ended October 31, 2002

*********************************************************

The Government announced today (November 30) its financial results for the seven months ended October 31, 2002.

Expenditure for the period April to October 2002 amounted to $139.2 billion and revenue to $66.8 billion, resulting in a deficit of $72.4 billion.

A Government spokesman explained that some major revenue items including salaries and profits taxes were mostly received towards the end of a financial year. It was too early at this stage to draw any conclusion on the deficit outturn for the full year.

The outturn would be affected mainly by two of our revenue items, namely land premium and sale of the second tranche of MTR shares. Subject to the outcome of other major revenue items, the budget deficit for 2002-03 could be larger than $45.2 billion estimated in March 2002.

The fiscal reserves stood at $300.1 billion at October 31, 2002.
