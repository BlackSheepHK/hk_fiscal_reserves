Financial results for ten months ended January 31, 2000

*******************************************************

The Government announced today (Tuesday) its financial results for the ten months ended January 31, 2000.

Expenditure for the period April 1999 to January 2000 amounted to $183,272.4 million and revenue to $161,553.3 million, resulting in a deficit of $21,719.1 million.

The fiscal reserves stood at $412,583.3 million at January 31, 2000, a decrease of $21,719.1 million compared with the balance of $434,302.4 million at March 31, 1999, the end of the previous financial year.

There was a surplus of $22,986.4 million in January. A Government spokesman said that the improvement in the finances was mainly due to the normal high level of Salaries Tax receipts during the month. An up-to-date assessment of the likely deficit for the current fiscal year will be provided by the Financial Secretary in his Budget Speech on March 8, 2000.

More detailed figures are shown in Tables 1 and 2.
