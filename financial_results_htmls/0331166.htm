Financial results for the eleven months ended Feb 1999

**************************************************

The government announced today (Wednesday), its results for the eleven months ended February 28, 1999.

Expenditure for the current fiscal year to date amounts to $210,392.1 million and revenue to $178,407.1 million, resulting in a deficit for the eleven months to February of $31,985 million. This is $4,504.4 million higher than that for the period to January. After taking account of the increase in the net worth of the Land Fund, the deficit for the year 1998-99 to end February 1999 was $23,679.9 million. The Financial Secretary announced in his Budget Speech on March 3, 1999 that he expected the deficit for 1998-99 to amount to $32.3 billion.

The fiscal reserves stood at $433,863.1 million as at February 28, 1999, a decrease of $23,679.9 million compared with the level as at March 31, 1998, the end of the previous financial year.

A government spokesman said that the results for the first eleven months reflect the effect of the package of special relief measures announced on 22 June 1998 as well as a general slow-down in revenue collections as a result of the economic downturn.
