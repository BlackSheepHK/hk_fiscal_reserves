Financial results for the nine months ended December 31, 2009
*********************************************************

     The Government announced today (January 29) its financial results for the nine months ended December 31, 2009.

     There was a surplus of $37.7 billion in the month of December, thereby reducing the deficit for the nine months ended December 31, 2009 to $1.2 billion.  Expenditure for the period amounted to $204.9 billion and revenue $203.7 billion.

     A government spokesman said the improved financial results in December were mainly due to the receipt of $33.5 billion investment income on fiscal reserves.  He said that having regard to the current financial position and that the outturn for some major revenue sources including land premium and stamp duties was likely to exceed the estimate, it was expected that the financial results for 2009-10 would be better than the original estimate.  The revised estimate for the current financial year will be published along with the 2010-11 Budget on February 24.

     The fiscal reserves stood at $493.1 billion as at December 31, 2009.
